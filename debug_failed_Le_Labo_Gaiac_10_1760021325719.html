Snooze-Ville
Gaiac 10 opens woodsy, with a bit of smoke wafting in the distance. As it dries, it passes through a detergent phase before it sweetens, becomes powdery & more musky. Wears close to the skin with mediocre longevity.

Although classified as a masculine, Gaiac 10 strikes me as unisex and it definitely can be worn by a gal. In fact, it may be more suited to women with its powdery & sweet character.

Overall, my experience is exactly what Sybarite says on another forum: "Wishy-washy woods, over wishy-washy incense and detergent musks." He nailed it. It's pleasant enough and not without interest, albeit a bit boring and common. At this price point ... snooze-ville.

Metallic sapling
This stuff has been my holy grail fragrance for over a year now since my Seattle trip last year, and I’m happy to say that now that I have a full bottle, it doesn’t disappoint. Gaiac 10 is a beautifully sharp green fragrance. It smells like the blade of an axe after cutting down a bunch of fresh, green, baby trees. It smells like you took a mercury bath and then had buckets of mowed grass dumped on you. It smells like you grabbed a scythe and went to town on a bush. It smells like you swam under a train bridge and a train car full of cleanly sliced rainforest vines tipped over onto you. It’s metallic, it’s green, its watery, its musky, it’s ever so slightly smokey, it’s got a hint of pepper, it’s clean, and it’s amazing. I know just last week I said that Kira Kira was the cleanest fragrance I owned but that was before this arrived so I’ll have to amend that, because the first time I wore this out to a friends house I got asked if I just took a shower. I love this stuff, but try before you buy.

I've tested Gaiac10 *many* times over many years, and I'm done giving it a chance. I am an ardent fan of many of Le Labo's scents (Santal33/ Rose31/ Baie Rose26/Jasmin17/Bergamote22....etc) , so my pocketbook is thankful that this one is a complete dud for me. Try before you buy!

Good BUT
I took a trip to Tokyo and got to sample this at one of the Le Labo stores in Japan. I really enjoyed it, but couldn’t justify the ridiculous price tag. I purchased a sample when I got home and I am so glad that I resisted buying a full bottle of Gaiac 10. It is a really nice woody/smoky scent that is subtle and pretty unique. However, in true Japanese fashion, this fragrance literally whispers off of the skin and disappears fairly quickly. For a fragrance that is more than $300, I’d have a hard time pulling the trigger on something I’m going to have to constantly re-apply during the day. If you have the means to purchase this and don’t mind dropping the coin, it is a lovely fragrance. It’s just too little juice, for too little performance, for too much coin for me.
