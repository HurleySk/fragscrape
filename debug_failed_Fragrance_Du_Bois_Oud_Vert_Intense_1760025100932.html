A longlasting, natural-smelling green masculine
Fresh and sharp, Oud Vert leaves us in no doubt as to the gender of its intended audience – no ‘wimmen’ allowed here, bud! The verdant sourness of bergamot leads the charge, given an almost animalic underbelly by way of freshly-peeled ginger root. The ginger means the opening runs perilously close to functional product territory but manages (just in time) to sidestep the reference by quickly burying the ginger in the minty wetness of geranium leaf.

The drydown, despite the numerous exotic spices listed, stays close to the aromatic citrus character set in motion by the opening. Overall, this is a bright, muscular citrus fragrance, with only a brief nod at the woodiness of oud or vetiver in the base and even then in the most oblique manner possible.

Those looking for a strong ‘oudy’ element may look elsewhere. But Oud Vert may prove to be the answer to that eternal question: where can I find a long-lasting citrus masculine for summer that doesn’t smell chemical and that doesn’t disappear into the ether within ten minutes? Of course, Oud Vert is not the answer if there is a budget cap. But if it is an open-ended query and your wallet is endlessly thick, there is no reason why this should not be choice number one or two on your list.

Beautiful fragrance, but in no way worth the price
Oud Vert Intense is another fragrance that was never a test candidate for me. Through a purchase in the souk I also got to this bottling. I knew before writing this review nothing at all about this perfume.

Oud Vert Intense is a nice fresh scent with vetiver, cedar, ginger, cardamom and some rose geranium, which is edged with a soft oud note. Oud Vert Intense is definitely interesting and I like it, but unfortunately it doesn't spark and the similarity to Cartier Déclaration is just too great. Cartier has just no oud note and is therefore something different. Unfortunately, the similarity is a bit too great for me. The oud note increasingly gains influence after a little over 3 hours, which I find a nice turn from rather fresh to something gently oriental.

The durability here is very good I could smell Oud Vert Intense over 10 hours and the silage is about 5-6 hours a little over an arm's length and then quickly becomes skin tight.

Unfortunately, I have now dared to look for the price. This was unfortunately not a good idea, I literally felt like I almost fell off the chair in shock. About 500€ for 100 Ml I find completely excessive and cheeky, because the fragrance is never worth it and there is also a great alternative here. I just can not close my eyes here and say I do not care, because such a price really brings very high expectations for me, but these can not meet Oud Vert Intense and in this case it is not entirely possible for me not to include the price in the rating and deduct a point from the fragrance for this.
