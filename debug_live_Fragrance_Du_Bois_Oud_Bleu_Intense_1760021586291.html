Earthy green 'blue' scent
Oud Bleu Intense is a fresh, spicy amber scent with a vaguely aquatic theme. It seems to derive its marine references from the naturally earthy nuances of myrrh and the cold green wateriness of cardamom, rather than the chemical awfulness of Calone or Ambroxan. Thank heavens for small mercies, I suppose.

The natural feel of Oud Bleu does not quite compensate for a general banality of composition or thinness of texture, however. A satisfying counterpart to this might be found on the shelves of any department store for less than half the price.

A very special perfume
Wasnt love at first sniff for me, definitely took me a while to understand and appreciate this scent, it is a resinous acquatic, as oxymoronic as this description sounds, the perfumer did a crazy good job blending this one and it is very multi faceted. I never thought of it being summer king, i always like to look at other scents for it but always find myself reaching for oud bleu. It became a very special scent for me and a signature and made so many memories with it throughout the past 3 years that i keep on wearing it. It is my 3rd bottle now, began with 15 mls then 50 and now 100ml and I can see myself wearing this scent for many years to come. It is very unexpected that this scent became my signature since I smelled it first time. Maybe because it is special and nothing smells like it? Maybe versatile? Maybe also quite sexy? Maybe just suits me? For all I know is that I always feel good wearing this scent and I recommend everyone go through a small decant as it does take a while to fully understand and appreciate the scent. Not for beginner noses. I've tried many scents throughout my journey and own and owned quite a few special scents but the 2 scents I always reach for with pleasure is oud bleu for day and roja parfum de la nuit 3 for special occasion, if i could only have 2 scents it would be these two.

SpeedDating #02/4: Bleu - wild & noble
Oud Bleu Intens brings herbaceous woody notes to the fore. Dried cabbage and tender minty nouances emphasize the green-fighting kind that oud can also have. Let me think of bitter-green and wild scents like Heeleys Phoenicia or the D.S. & Durga's, but after a short time they are surrounded by warm, good-natured wood.

Almost salty, mineraly-stony and clearly herbaceous, the fragrance reminds me of classic men's perfumes, and tart and freshly scented men's department stores. Citrus fruits, peat and whiskey complete the autumnal freshness, painting a picture of colourful green deciduous forests, already roamed by the first cold winter air. Creaky, almost bitter sage and sour woods illuminate the green side of oud extensively without losing the heat glowing in the depths. Supported by the resinous-harsh and yet buttery-smooth olofactory resin Opoponax, the scent always remains between wildness and classic seriousness, always between cool minerals and warming heart...
