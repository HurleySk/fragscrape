Nothing went right here
For a brand as big as Creed, to get an original IP as wrong as this is quintessential corporate perfumery. Carmina opens with a rose that is quite watery/fresh, flanked by the same powdered cherry tonality recognizable in scents like Duchessa. There is no sweetness to help out and as such, the top accord is floral based on the opening. The saffron doesn't really pop at all for me throughout the wearing experience.

However, it is the drydown that I find SUPER BIZARRE and not in a good way - there is a very prominent synthetic feel to this scent. The blend quality is very lackluster and leaves the scent structured like it's from Mancera. I can't figure out what is messing so much with my nose here, but I am thinking it is the blend of amber & musk in the base that ends up very prominent when smelling the scent from up-close. The myrrh/frankincense slightly tickle my nose and add mild warmth to the scent.

I just can't get over the poor blend quality and as such, Carmina goes into the bottom 10 among my 2023 rankings. The performance on this scent was horrible - within 3.5-4 hours we are talking about trace amounts. There's so little left that the deep drydown smells a little bit powdery & minty, dare I say slightly berry-like (so completely different from the core scent). For the first 1-1.5 hours, sillage might be a 6/10 but I'll deduct a point since it's not expected to remain above intimate after hour 2-2.5.

Dark Horse Perfume
Black cherry opens immediately in Carmina with a tenacity that persists throughout the longevity of the perfume, and it adapts and compliments each set of notes that unfold. Saffron and pink pepper add an underlying spice that appeals to my nose, an earthy roundness that balances the cherry's sweetness. There is the tiniest whisper of nostalgia here for me to, it's evocative of the memory of the legacy of my great-grandparents' love story.

When the middle notes come in with their powdery softness, the top notes don't so much fade as they evolve. The may rose and peony are fresh and airy, delicate, creating a romantic contrast to the framing of the spicy-sweet top notes and sexy, sensual, richness of the dry-down. The violet has this almost candied blooming quality, and with the velvety sophistication of cashmere wood adding a woody, warm, smoothness, the musky incense and amber only deepen this perfume.

I'm a big fan of the dry-down here as it never fully takes over the other notes for me, it simply adds one more layer of complexity. I wonder whether the black cherry itself could be better, since even though I enjoy it, some find it quite medicinal. As mentioned earlier, the amber, musk, and incense are sexy, and the ambroxan plays so perfectly with my skin. At this point, the sweetness becomes a whisper, and the narcotic florals are deepened like a secret that wants to be found out. I would adore this on a man as much as I enjoy wearing this on myself.

I would probably keep a decant of this in my collection for special occasions, going out, and moody nights. I'd love it as a gift, but I would probably never buy a full bottle.
