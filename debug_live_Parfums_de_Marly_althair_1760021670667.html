Warm and comforting vanilla
Althaïr by Parfums de Marly is a soft, comforting fragrance. Vanilla, of course, is the main star here but the other player is a woods and musk accord that is reminiscent of fresh laundry.

Opening with a good amount of citrus in the form of bergamot, Althaïr gives me a bit of Ani Extrait de Parfum vibes when it is first sprayed. Citrus, some spices and florals to round it out, with the vanilla in the background. However, that’s where the similarities end with the Nishane fragrance. While Ani heads deeper into the gourmand space, Althaïr introduces the aforementioned woods and musk accord, listed as guaiac wood, musk, and ambroxan in the note pyramid. I am reminded of Creed Original Santal or Montblanc Individuel Eau de Toilette but without the eponymous sandalwood. Instead the creaminess is provided by vanilla and the floral notes, listed as orange blossom. The vanilla itself is described as "bourbon" vanilla and it does give some boozy or tobacco tones. Although vanilla is the main note, I wouldn't really describe this as a gourmand fragrance per se. For me, the cinnamon and praline are not that prominent, and the scent profile is better described as woody and sweet.

It is a powerful fragrance. Projects well over 5-6 hours and lasts all day on the skin. Overall, really happy with my blind buy! It seems I’m enjoying all of Hamid Merati-Kashani’s creations of late. I even got a sample of Pegasus Exclusif with this purchase that is definitely full bottle worthy!

Best of the Worst of the Top 50
For context: There isn't a single fragrance in the top 50 "men's" list on this site that I can even tolerate, let alone enjoy. I dislike them all, except this one.

On first spray I get an opening that reminds me of the medicinal herbals of Brecourt's Mauvais Garcon with the same fresh aromatic opening and underlying depth of spice, amber, and vanilla. On the one hand cozy, on the other hand clinical. This provides a composition that allows you to experience both warmth and cleanliness at once. The contrasting nature of the layers only appears when sprayed for me, and it opens into this clearly intended juxtaposition of the two.

This is the kind of fragrance where I don't mind a few powdery notes, a note I usually dislike because it smells like baby wipes or powder to me personally - but when it's in a composition like this it becomes a binding agent bringing together the herbal and sugary notes of a fragrance. I don't think it's especially unique and it has the same profile as a lot of others, it's a safe scent that doesn't stand out. It's acceptable, nice even, and though not interesting to me I don't dislike it - which is saying something for one of the top 50 in men's perfumes.
