Is very expensive also = very good ?
I think the Seastar collection really splits the minds here the price is really almost cheeky is no Roja (irony) and the flacons with the OVP's almost wasteful. I say here right away that I could buy Cubia from a very nice Parfumo and have not paid the UVP, now but long enough the reader bored here, we come to the most important the fragrance itself: I smell after spraying directly a fruit explosion, the indicated notes in the fragrance pyramid can be smelled really well and especially the peach, orange and currants, after about 2-3 hours join woods to it I take here sandalwood was and a slight incense note. The statement about the big mess in the bottle I can not really understand, because for me the fragrance a very well perceivable scent progression and smells for me also not really messed up, as I have really experienced much worse. The only criticism here is that the top note could be perceived a little clearer and here a little more orange would not have hurt, but this is just my personal opinion, but now it's time to clarify the most important question is the perfume worth the high RRP? I would say yes under the following points: I give the perfume a 10/10, love pompous OVP's and I find the normal expensive perfumes too boring like Roja, Xerjoff etc. I for one am unfortunately not one of the absolute fans of this perfume. Cubia is really very good and an incredibly great perfume, but unfortunately not a 10/10 and for such a price the fragrance has to be at least 10/10 for me to justify in any way buying a perfume for over 400€. I would find 200€ acceptable for Cubia, but the estimated price is really too much in my opinion, but ultimately everyone must decide for themselves whether he or she is willing to pay the price for Cubia and I leave for the rating the price also outside, because this would distort the rating.
