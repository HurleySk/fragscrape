Addictive raspberries on smokey leather
Every time I tested this on paper or just smelled the atomizer on my sample I thought Sekushi was just a rough, smokey, dry leather scent with a vague, barely there berry note. I'm so glad I gave this a chance on my skin and also kept some of the paper blotters around for a little longer, because it unfolds into something beautiful and interestingly blended.

Yes, the leather is quite prominent at first, and it feels cold, a little coarse even. But the raspberry actually comes in rather quickly and takes the scent to a new level. These two opposite notes work so well together. I get patchouli and a sense of fizziness with the raspberry, and I also smell a warm, lactonic apricot. Now the leather note works as an interesting backdrop for the sweeter notes and keeps the fragrance from turning into another generic fruitchouli. The frankincense is not balsamic here, it smells more like cold smoke in stone church walls, which emphasizes the coolness of the leather note.
I wouldn't clock this as a cotton candy scent, even though it's definitely sweet. It just doesn't smell like freshly caramelized sugar at the fair to me, it's more of a creamy and fruity kind of sweetness, blended with a subtle skin musk.

All in all I love this. It's feminine, sexy and playful, oscillating between sweet and dark. Original without being abrasive, and a very welcome fresh perspective on my favorite fruit, the raspberry.

Spring at last!
For me it reflects a perfect warm spring day, when it's not too sunny but pleasantly warm and the lilies of the valley are blooming in the meadow.

But first to No 7 - Sekushi Extrait de Parfum. The No 7 - Sekushi Extrait de Parfum smells quite strongly of raspberry underlined by a sweet apricot, which makes it super fruity. The cotton candy makes the scent a bit sweeter, but not too sweet because the musk, leather and rosewood combined with the incense don't make it too sweet. It also has a floral touch thanks to the lily of the valley.

It is a fruity, spicy, floral and leathery fragrance that is perfectly balanced. In my opinion, the fragrance is simply the more floral and less feminine 17 Rosso Limited Edition and I find both fragrances very beautiful.

My conclusion: Definitely a spring banger you should try if you like fruity, floral fragrances.
