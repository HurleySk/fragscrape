More like Mr Mistoffelees...

I have to admit that the trail this perfume leaves behind is more heavenly than evil...
The citrus opening is sophisticated, slightly sweet and very refined, taking the wearer clearly into niche perfumery territory. The journey continues with a transition to a delicate but well-defined floral phase, in which the notes gradually emerge and remain in the composition for the longest part of the olfactory experience. The base is slightly woody, without ever losing the citrus and floral notes of the previous phases.

The staying power is excellent and lasts for 6/8 hours.

I think I leaned more towards the masculine side, but definitely towards a delicate and gentle masculinity. No aggression here...

Helpful Review 4  
An elegant citrus rose perfume.
Xerjoff Mefisto is a delightful green-citrus floral perfume, with a dominant rose accord. I bought a full bottle recently after testing a sample from Xerjoff. I rate Mefisto as one of the best citrus florals available.

I am amazed how beautiful the opening citrus notes are. It is more green than other citrus scents because of the bergamot. But this green is tempered by other brighter citrus notes (lemon and grapefruit) and a hint of lavender in the opening. In addition, the floral notes from the middle make an appearance almost immediately. These middle floral notes in Mefisto are rose and iris, not the white floral notes that typically come with other green citrus perfumes. After a few hours on the skin, the citrus notes in Mefisto completely disappear into the background and the rose becomes dominant. This rose really dominates and defines the personality of this perfume. The base notes - amber, musk, cedar and sandalwood - are subtle and harmonious, as the base notes tend to be weaker in intensity. So, I hesitate to call this a woody scent.

The longevity is very good with more than ten hours on the skin, and a little bit more on clothes. Mefisto shows very good sillage, a common characteristic of most Xerjoff scents.

In summary, this is an affordable green-citrus-rose perfume, yet it is very versatile. I like to rotate it with Nishane Colognise to enjoy the similarity and contrast. Colognise is similar but the dominant floral notes are lily of the valley and neroli. I strongly recommend Mefisto if you are looking for a citrus rose perfume.
