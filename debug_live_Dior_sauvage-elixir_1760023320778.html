Sauvage Family of Fragrances - Excellent and Versatile
This is a combined review of Sauvage EDT, EDP, Parfum and Elixir after having tested all four on consecutive days. The Sauvage line, in general, is very popular for a reason. These are excellent, masculine fragrances that are very versatile, working any time of the day, any day of the year, hot or cold or in between. They are also very people pleasing in that most who smell these perfumes on a guy will say they are pleasant scents and attractive. Thus, they are all an easy grab for most situations. They also work for most men of any age. Dior quality is evident in all four perfumes.

There is a concern about the popularity and not standing out with them or being unique. I can't address that aspect because I live in a rural area where the vast majority of men do not wear any fragrance at all. So, no problem for me.

Regarding how different the four perfumes are from each other, I'd say the Sauvage DNA is quite clearly shared in the EDT, EDP and Parfum while the Elixir is more distant. As an analogy, I'd say the EDT, EDP and Parfum are like brothers while the Elixir is a cousin. Still, the EDT, EDP and Parfum are sufficiently unique that it isn't redundant to own all three if you like the different aspects of each.

To sum up these aspects, the EDT is more citrusy, the EDP is smoother, rounder and creamier than the EDT while the Parfum is a more woody version of the EDP. The Elixir is a more warm spicy fragrance with touches of cinnamon and licorice.

If a guy is unsure about which to get, I highly recommend purchasing samples of all four and wearing each on separate consecutive days. That's what I did. This is a much better approach than sniffing all four on test strips in a store. My personal choice is to go with the EDP and the Elixir as I do not have a goal to own all four just for the sake of a complete collection. Of course this is subjective, but the EDP and the Elixir were the most appealing to me.

Performance of the EDT, EDP and Parfume are all quite good with the EDP having the best performance for me. The Elixir is in a class by itself - a truly beast mode fragrance that requires experimentation to determine the quantity to apply. It can easily become overwhelming if too much is applied. The Elixir allows you to fill a room and leave a trail for sure!

The Stuff of Nightmares to Classic Perfumery Lovers...
I won't waste any time writing an extensive review with note breakdown of this horrific stinker. Projection is stellar and so is longevity at 20 terrifying hours on skin despite several attempts to wash it off.

While terrified by how bad a release the original Sauvage was, I read enough folks say that Sauvage Elixir was worth a try even if you disliked the original. Well, now having purchased and wearing a sample of Elixir, I think pretty much if you enjoyed the synthetic ambroxan smell of regular Sauvage, you will near-certainly enjoy the overpowering strong, highly synthetic profile of this one too. If your tastes run to more along mine (think 70s and 80s natural smelling powerhouses) steer well-clear of this abomination and insult to the world of perfume. The bottom line is Sauvage Elixir, like its Edt counterpart is scary, nightmarish stuff indeed, "earning" a 1 star out of 5 "horrific" rating and a mega-avoid recommendation. Seek out the spectacular classic *Eau* Sauvage Edt instead, says I.

It`s not weak, Your nose may be exhausted!
I have purchased the Elixir after a whole day smelling testers in perfumeries but could not find the Elixir in stock. In the evening I finally found one and bought it, after I smelled the tester and loved it. Once I got home, I opened the box and sprayed it again on my wrist. After, an hour, I could not smell it anymore, so I panicked and thought i may have bought a bad batch. I have the 100ml bottle and production was from 8 months ago.

I gave it sometime, left it closed in the closet. After few weeks, I have decided to try it again, and men, the smell was strong and longevity was for hours. I also noticed, where I sprayed it, the skin had like an oily patch form the spray, meaning its very concentrated. I had a shower (did not scrub my wrist but water went on it), and the day after I could still smell through my wrist and smell the beautiful scent, obviously weak but still there.

Sometimes, we smell so many perfumes, in a short amount of time that our noses go blind and immediately decide that the batch is wrong. Give your nose a break, you`ll see the difference.

I had a couple of discounts and managed to buy it for around Eur 160 for the 100ml bottle from a good reliable perfumery in Europe.

The scent is masculine, warm, strong, sexy and elegant at the same time, not elegant as much as DHI but in a different way. I`m glad I bought it, not sure how many occasions I have to be able to wear it but was worth the price and the scent is unique.

Obviously, this is only my personal opinion.

Overly spicy, borderline offensive to the wearer
As i said in my title, hard to wear for me. I loved it when i first tried it, albeit it was on clothes. Now as the time goes on and it macerated i feel it is hard to pull off. It is very spicy and very masculine and it doesnt allow me to relax if i spray more than one spray on my neck. Especially if there is more than 15 degrees celsius outside. I used up 30% of the bottle pretty quickly but now, a year later, struggle to reach 50%, and it is a 60mL… i dont ever do a full press spray on it. I do two half sprays on my neck and one on clothes. As far as the scent goes, pretty well blended with prominent licorice, nutmeg and creamy woods for me. If it doesnt offend you it could work in all seasons except summer. It is still a nice scent with undeniable performance. Price is on the expensive side, however with many frags that are more expensive having far worse performance ill give it a pass. As far as the line goes, EDT is my winner.
