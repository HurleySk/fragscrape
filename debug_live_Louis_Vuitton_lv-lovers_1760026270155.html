Good But Not Great
I like it but I definitely don’t love it.

This resembles a bit of Gris Charnel Eau de Parfum in combination with Santal 33 Eau de Parfum.

The opening is a nice galbanum with a hint of ginger. I don’t know if it’s because the weather is 99° F where I live but the green goodness from the galbanum literally disappear within a few minutes.

The drydown comes rather fast. A creamy sandalwood and zesty ginger will sit close for awhile.

On my skin I do not get the “dill” scent as some mentioned, but for whatever reason, I get a fresh scent as if I was sitting on my porch. Is it the solar notes? Possibly.
If this was a little more on the original side, I would purchase it, but it reminds me of other fragrances and that’s what I try and avoid.

Sample it because you might like it. Who knows, if I find a secondhand bottle for a great price, I might pick it up.

A Softer and Pleasant Ginger over Sandalwood
I find the repeated comparisons to Santal 33 misleading and somewhat reductionist. Obviously, this has a sandalwood base but it is a far softer and also more complex scent than Santal 33. I compared my Santa 33 to it across two days right side/left side and it's simply not the same (at least on me). I do understand how it is rather as if one combined Gris Charnel and Santal 33 but those two together would be rougher; this is quieter and more subtle. The quality ingredients seem more refined and luxurious than either of those individually, much less combined (and I own both of the others). What I liked a best about it was the lovely ginger note which is prominent at the start and remains through hours of drydown. The duration of this on me is about 4-5 hours. I wouldn't say I love it but that's because I personally have moved away from sandalwood over the past few years. I have no doubt, however, that were I to want to add another sandalwood base parfum to my collection, this would be a quality addition.

Lovely Lovers
A fresh, bold, modern green scent that is pleasant and uplifting. I do perceive a bit of the "pickle" accord that is mentioned, it's a little odd at first but it quickly subsides into a very bright and sparkly wood. At first sniff, I can see how someone would compare it to Santal 33, but after getting used to it, it's definitely different. It's less woody and more citrusy but still very balanced, while Santal 33 is a lot more wood forward.

Overall, I quite enjoy it. It's extremely unoffensive, and very versatile. Leans ever-so-slightly feminine, but as a man I still can rock it. It's not quite as god-tier as some of the iconic LV scents, but it's certainly a worthy entry. It doesn't last very long, however. It's not something I would buy a whole bottle of but I'm enjoying the decant I have and would consider getting another decant sometime.
