The best Tom Ford !!!
In 2018, Tom Ford created Fougère d'Argent, which for me personally is one of the top 3 candidates in the entire Private Blend series.
The Moss Breches from 2007 was still unknown to me, like so many old fragrances from the house, so I took a closer look at the Moss Breches because I really liked the fragrance notes and I could imagine that it could take a similar direction to the Fougère d'Argent.
I kept hearing stories like "everything used to be better" and "the old Fords were milestones" and so on and so "Ford"... but I only found out that many of these Ford's went "ford" (ok enough now) when I took a closer look at the treasured treasures.
The fact that this one, like the other good old "milestones" from the series, was discontinued was not yet the tip of the iceberg.
So I had been looking in vain for a sample or a bottle from reputable retailers for some time. When one appeared on EBay and I saw the prices listed for one or two isolated bottles of Moss Breches, I rubbed my eyes.
I closed the Moss Breches chapter for the time being Just under a thousand euros for a 50ml bottle...with all due respect...they're just fragrances.
I'm sure there are a few freaks who would pay these prices,
but I'm not one of them Only a smaller bottling would be an option for me.
I have already been able to test some old "milestones" of the Fords, although not all of them, but purely from the fragrance notes, states and reviews, not all of them appealed to me either, so it's not so bad But that was different with the Moss Breches, which I'd had my eye on for a long time A perceived gold treasure that I would never get to see or put under my nose...I couldn't accept that.
One day, I took another look at it, just out of boredom. It was still this one fragrance that sounded the most interesting to me.
And let's be honest, fragrance treasures make it even more interesting.
So I started my search again and found a very trustworthy and valued Parfumo member with whom I exchanged ideas on the subject.
After careful and polite inquiries, he finally allowed me to test this fragrance experience.
Long story short, expensive fragrance.
Let's get to the fragrance... not only is it uniquely expensive, it also smells like it.
My first association was a notch to my 2018 batch of Fougère d'Argent.
I thought to myself, that's exactly how I imagined it.
But here it's clearly different again.
These two seem related While the Fougère d'Argent is for me a green-woody, slightly sweet and creamy dense fougere with a subtle synthetic through the Akigalawood, the Moss Breches is a slightly lighter oriental and incredibly natural smelling fougere fragrance.In the later drydown they are almost identical, but the Moss Breches smells much more multifaceted from the beginning and is a touch more delicate and also a tad sweeter than the Fougère d'Argent The beeswax in here is so authentic and world class, it is definitely the main player in the fragrance It gives the fragrance that certain twist that makes it slightly cinnamony.
The fragrance manages a unique balancing act between light and dark, between fresh and heavy and between day and night.
Except in midsummer, it is always wearable.
But I see it similar to the Fougère d'Argent rather in the fall / winter.
It is also not a pure date fragrance but rather an event fragrance. An elegant gentleman's fragrance which I will only wear on special occasions due to the price and the quantity available.
I would like to thank Anthracite once again for the bottling and the opportunity to have this unique fragrance experience under my nose.
Because this is a Tom Ford as it is written in the book.

Well, I like him, the TOM
I generally love Tom's fragrances and the durability is enormous, as is the silage The bottles always remind me of pharmacy bottles. I love these jars.

So, now to "Moss Breches". I pronounce it the way you spell it and it sounds terrible.
What kind of fragrance would you expect?
When I smell Moss Breches, it immediately reminds me of the 80s/90s. There used to be a similar fragrance. Unfortunately, I can't find it at the moment.
It's described as oriental - seriously? For me, it smells more like fuzzy bed linen, a tousled Tom Hardy from LEGEND *swoon* and a 3-pass sauna session.
It smells kind of juicy, dirty and kind of good like a lamb. Powdery and wet at the same time.
Clary sage and labdanum, as well as patchouli and moss, certainly give it the dirtiness. And beeswax sticks it all together a little so that nothing leaks or runs away. Unfortunately, I can't smell rosemary, although that would certainly be a special kick, because rosemary with its essential oils is supposed to stimulate the mind - at least for me - so it probably didn't really work here ;-)
There aren't that many spices and I can't really say what they might be, but they give Moss Breches something homely and familiar. And fresh wood, yes, more towards the end, when the bed is freshly made.
I like it, it envelops you, as if you are being hugged, without distance, but pressed firmly against your chest. However, I would see it more as a magnet for women. It is certainly divine on a man. Ok, it also depends a little on the situation *laughs*
