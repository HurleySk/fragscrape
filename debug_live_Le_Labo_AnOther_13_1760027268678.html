Another Ambroxan Bottled as Niche Perfume
Why does Le Labo ANOTHER 13 smell so familiar to me? Maybe because it is essentially a base in near ubiquity at this point in perfume history. Ambroxan plus some musk. And, for heaven's sake, don't forget the iso-E-super!

Okay, so I already sniffed both Escentric Molecules MOLECULE 02 and Juliette Has A Gun NOT A PERFUME. Been there, done that. Don't need a bottle, that's for sure, but I have a generous decant from Guusje.

I'll keep it on hand, in case I "forget" what ambroxan smells like. In fact, that seems quite unlikely to happen, as I feel that through this three-part exercise I am now capable of recognizing ambroxan bases in more complex creations.

I do hope that Le Labo will now return to making perfumes.

So faint as to be undetectable on me
I received the full official Le Labo sample set as a gift, and I’ve been having fun trying them out. But I think of Another 13 and Ambrette 9 as the two “ghost perfumes” of the lot. They are both so faint as to be almost undetectable on my skin. What I can manage to smell is nice, but it’s barely there. I have no idea at all why; the rest are perfectly strong and distinguishable from each other. Even the ones I’m not so keen on at least smell like SOMETHING. But these two? Ghosts.

I see from previous comments that others have had the same experience. Some speculate it’s the ISO E Super, and report similar effects with JHAG’s Not A Perfume and MFK’s Baccarat Rouge 540. For me, though, Not A Perfume smells like hot garbage on my skin, yet BR540 smells delightful. So I don’t know if it is the ISO E Super!

Ah, skin chemistry...what a mystery you are. Still, it’s what keeps things interesting!

Mother of BR540
The mother of Baccarat Rouge 540? I think so...I do not know any other fragrance that combines an overdose of Ambrox/Cetalox or a variation as such with Veramoss IFF (oakmoss) and Hedione (transparent jasmine or white flower petals) at this level prior to this one so gotta give credits to Nathalie Lorson for coming up with such minimal yet likeable creation in 2010. I always respect the pioneering blends a lot even if they couldn't catch the sweet moments in perfumery like BR540 going viral.

You can just tell by the reviews here: 40+ matches for "BR540" or "Baccarat", but if you go to BR540's page, there is around 5 mentions of Another 13 which suggests people are taking BR540 as the baseline, I say it should be the other way around...But that's what social media and word of mouth does to you.

I share this with my spouse...
I gifted my husband the le labo discovery set on a whim last year as he needed some help in the cologne department. We spent time and worked our way through every scent in the house, and this was hands down our favorite.

It is fresh and lives on the skin so beautifully. It melds into my skin totally different than it does my husband, but we both receive compliments when wearing.

On me it smells fresh and light. I think the pear and jasmine notes really project when I wear it. It smells light and almost aquatic on me. I love it as an everyday scent.

On my husband it pulls more musky and the green notes come through. There is also some pepperiness to it that pulls it masculine with his skin chemistry. My husband swears to him Another 13 reminds him of riding Pirates of the Caribbean at Disney. I kind of get it, like a musky dark aquatic on him.

We kept stealing each others samples and finally ended up gifting each other full bottles for Christmas, so now we each have our own! I reach for it all the time. It is my husband's signature scent right now, and one of my preferred every day scents.

I saw somewhere that people compared or saw hints of BR540 in another 13, and I don't get that at all on me. On me it seems much more adjacent to fresh scents like Acqua di Gioia EDT, but there is also something in Another 13 that gives it more depth.

This scent lasts on me and my clothes for days. I do think it becomes more of a skin scent after 6 hours or so. But I definitely think it is unique enough to have in your collection.
