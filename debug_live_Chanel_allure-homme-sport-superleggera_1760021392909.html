Fragrance Review :
Fragrance Notes

Top Notes:
Citrus, Mandarin, Grapefruit.

Mid Notes:
Woody Notes, Cedarwood.

Base Notes:
White Musk, Patchouli, Amber, Sandalwood.

Fragrance Review

First thoughts, this is an aromatic/citrus fragrance for men, similar to Allure Homme Sport.

- Superleggera starts off very citrus, similar DNA to Allure Homme Sport with a subtle hint of Lavender and Aldehyde notes. Creating a lighter/airy version of Allure Homme Sport. The mid and drydown consists of Musk, and warm spicy patchouli note.

- I sprayed a total of 5 times. Twice behind each ear, twice on the front of neck, and once in front of my chest. The longevity is good, it lasts approximately 7-9 hours on my skin, projects for about an arms length for 30 minutes to 45 minutes before turning into a skin scent. It does however lasts a few days on my clothes.
(Depends on skin types)

Overall Rating (8/10)

An ideal fragrance for the summer/warm weather, it's a safe blind buy if you enjoyed the original Allure Homme Sport DNA. Quite an interesting, lighter version with a nice touch of amber warmth in the drydown.

This 100ml/3.3 FL oz bottle was purchased for £130/$160.

First impression of the new Allure Homme Sport Superleggera....
Hello everyone,

you can rely on the company..., maybe we have a little upcoming star here.

I received a sample yesterday for Sunday from "a fragrance buddy" and tested it today + yesterday (at work, while sleeping and in my free time) and would like to give you (m)a little impression.

It contains so much of the Chanel palette and yet so little...

What exactly do I mean by that?

You can tell quite "clearly" where it comes from, in the sense of the Allure Homme Sport Eau de Toilette with its sparkling, fresh, light and the Allure Homme Édition Blanche Eau de Parfum and a beautiful lemon (one of the most beautiful top notes I know) and by the way you can also notice the Bleu de Chanel Eau de Toilette, but not purely from the scent, but from the massive FLAVORFULNESS!
Chanel has never positively shocked us with a pure performance, but it was just the pleasantness and the certain quality in the price level and so it is here!

The fragrance is very similar to the bottle - transparent, soft, doesn't offend and that's how it is! A fresh and pleasant musk bomb! At the end in the drydown, a light and subtle woody creaminess comes through, but it is so subtle that you almost have to look for it.

Generally soooo beautiful, pleasant, clean = you feel like you've had a fresh shower!
How is it received?

Typical for Chanel = insanely good!
Where and when?

Almost everywhere, whether at work, while shopping, during and after sports or just like that ;) . I see it more in the months of March to October! In winter, of course, it also works very well and YES it harmonizes very well with Molecule 01, then it probably has enough power for the winter.

Performance:

Perfectly ok and sufficient for a 7h or 8h working day and this he also manages, but you notice after 6h it is already tight^^in the sense of skin scent. And the sillage is really good for just under 30 minutes, after that it goes back noticeably fast!

But that was already the case with the Allure Homme Eau de Toilette + Allure Homme Édition Blanche Eau de Parfum and it doesn't bother me (at least not me).

Can I recommend it?

Let me put it this way = you can hardly do anything wrong if you test it. Then decide whether you want to buy it or not.

And would I buy it?

I wouldn't be surprised if it found its way to me ;)

And now have a nice day/evening, or whatever you read this review, LG the Benny

Creamy Stuff
A new Chanel men's release? In 2024? Bring it on! "Superleggera" is a successful, albeit somewhat safe combination of "Allure Homme Sport" and the "Edition Blanche" - and the latter in particular delights me a little...

"Superleggera" has everything a high-quality designer workhorse needs. It works wonderfully in leisure and fitness, from the office to the beer tent. Chanel's fresh, citrusy men's DNA of recent years, which some have been putting in the pantydropper camp for what feels like forever. But now much creamier and... cakey? More tart? And without being a real gourmand? Yes, that works. Channel has already tested this extensively. It might be too sweet, soft and waffly for the players and influencers of this world. It doesn't directly pull every Schlübbi down. But for me, it's a great fragrance. And it's probably the same for some perfumes that care about the scent and themselves - not just about external impact, prestige, followers, hypes and pillow talk. Musk pops pleasantly through. The grapefruit is not too reminiscent of deodorants and shower gels. A slight saltiness is probably the best way to assign it its own identity. Otherwise, safe and sound and smell.

Flacon: Chanel standard
Sillage: with suspicion of compliments
Longevity: doesn't end so quickly... 8 hours!

Conclusion: not a gamechanger from Channel... but a delicious, fluffy combination of some of their bestsellers of the last two decades. "Superleggera" can do something, but is more of a best-of. It lacks a little character of its own. At a high level.
