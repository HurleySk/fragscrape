Les vacances en Tahiti
This has to be the best yellow-floral fragrance. When I spray this, it always transports me to an exotic place. It has natural ingredients and that shows, because it doesn’t smell at all synthetic- I find Fragrance du Bois fragrances to be the most “natural” smelling fragrances I’ve tried. So if you don’t like the synthetic feel of Mancera and Montale, this niche house may not dissapoint you.
As for Oud Jaune Intense- It’s the fragrance of an exotic goddess with monoi flowers in her hair, laying on the beach, sunbathing and eating tropical fruits - that’s how it smells to me.
I always wear it on my summer vacation.
As for longevity and projection, I get 8-10 hours of this juice on my skin, with a strong sillage in the first 2 hours. Also, note that I wear it only when the temperature is pretty high ( 30* Celsius or more)- and that may influence it strenght.

Tropical Jasmine scent where nothing bothers me
First a short description of the scent for those who can't bear to read the whole review:

This is a blend of tropical fruits, creamy texture from Ylang-Ylang, fascinating white florals and a little hint of Coconut like aroma which is reminiscent of sun screen lotion. It's bright and joyous, somehow magical scent which works best in hot weather. As for the name, there is no detectable oud in the scent, but the same problem exists in other fragrances from Fragrance du Bois where Oud is mentioned in the name. "Jaune intense" is justified, as the yellow flower Ylang-Ylang is clearly represented in the scent with its rich, sweet-sour and banana-like aroma. It beautifully intertwines with sensual non heady Jasmine and sweetish Orange Blossom, both of which remain non animalic. The scent lasts and projects strongly and may be disturbing to those around you, especially indoors.

About other tropical white florals:

I have had a decant of this one for 3 years now and I still haven't decided whether I need it or not. I've noticed that the longer time goes by, the less interested I am in this scent, although I still like it. In reality, Oud Jaune Intense was a special and unique scent when it was released, even three years ago the market was not full of tropical, creamy, white florals. Yet even today I think it ranks well in comparisons of similar scents, because I often find something unpleasant in scents like this that I just can't stand. Some scents which I could describe as "creamy, tropical, white floral" and which are good, are Vanille Riviera , Jump Up and Kiss Me Ecstatic , Isola Sol , Queen of Silk , Amethyst Soul and this one, Oud Jaune Intense Parfum each one interesting in its own way.

Background:

My little note after testing this scent (three years ago): "This I like a lot, really intoxicating and addictive. There is Jasmin, orange blossom and I like them. For summer time definitely! Compare the overall vibe to Ecstatic Clive Christian (but this is better)."

At that time I didn't have such a huge number of fragrances and the idea was to only buy 1-2 full bottles per category, so I thought I would choose one of them: Oud Jaune Intense or Jump Up and Kiss Me Ecstatic , even though the scents are not the same at all and Ecstatic being mainly a complex Tuberose fragrance. Despite my first impressions I only bought the latter one a few months ago and after hundreds, or rather well over a thousand fragrances, I am convinced that Ecstatic is still a very special and exceptionally beautifully made fragrance. Oud Jaune Intense, on the other hand, doesn't speak to me in the same way anymore and its quality is in no way comparable to that of Ecstatic albeit it has its own advantages.

Thank you for reading and I hope my review was helpful.

Cash grab yellow floral
I genuinely do not understand the existence of this perfume. With its combination of tiaré, ylang, and pineapple, it smells so close to Yves Rocher Monoï Oil or, heaven forefend, Amarige, that you begin to wonder if it is just impossible for any perfumer, no matter how skilled, to throw these particular materials together and have them not land in the same place. If you are into those King Kong-sized fruit punch florals, and have the money to indulge yourself, then Oud Jaune might turn out to be your personal idea of heaven. For the rest of us, a similar effect is almost guaranteed via the ten-times-cheaper Yves Rocher, or failing that, any European tanning oil. If you insist on niche, believing it to be intrinsically superior to mainstream stuff, then something like Armani Privée Rouge Malachite or one of the Tom Ford Soleil de something or other should scratch the same itch.

Very nice fragrance, but...
... in my opinion, the name doesn't fit the fragrance at all.
I definitely don't smell intense yellow oud.
Yes, you could assign the color yellow to the top note because of the pineapple and the yellow flowers ylang ylang and monoi.
And it is definitely intense, very intense in fact.
This morning I sprayed 4 (high-quality) niche fragrances on both the inside and outside of my wrists and tested them.
One fragrance won me over right from the start with its strong sillage, long wear and beautiful scents. It was the Oud Jaune Intense Parfum
As I said... the fragrance opens tropical, fruity, summery fresh and delicately floral. A fragrance for warm spring days, balmy summer evenings, vacations on the beach.
What else did I sniff out?
Very subtle white flowers, by no means a typical white-flower scent and no excessive jasmine note.
Water fruits...? If that means juicy tropical fruits like pineapple, melon etc., then I agree.
Vanilla and musk remain discreetly in the background, but give the fragrance a pleasant depth and a creamy, clean base.
I would describe the fragrance as fairly linear. The top note is simply very dominant here and you notice it with every movement.
What did I not sniff out?
Oud.
Thank goodness
I can recommend this fragrance to anyone who likes tropical, summery, fresh, luxurious scents.
The price is hefty, but there is a 50ml bottle and it will definitely last a long time, because even I (frequent sprayer!) only need 2 to 4 sprays.
It's been on my wish list for a while and I think it will be here by the summer.
