Repackaged & Revised
For those of you who aren't aware, Sweetie Aoud has been repackaged and revised along with many other Roja fragrances. Sweetie Aoud previously had a cursive font on its gold plating and came in a white box with the traditional satin padding inside. Now the bottle has standard font (CAPS) and comes in a black box that I will just say does not travel well when shipped.

I almost never enjoy reformulations, but this most recent era of Sweetie Aoud is actually an improvement. I liked the original formula and it was a strong like, but it just lacked performance and did not wear like a Parfum should. Otherwise it was a great fragrance. It is now fuller and dances on the skin beautifully, with added projection. How is it different? Sweetie Aoud (cursive font) contained oud, but was really more about olibanum (frankincense) and didn't allow the oud to really shine. I also found that I needed to wait before I could really enjoy the fragrance. Sweetie Aoud (standard font) is more true to its title, while still conserving that patisserie shop vibe. The updated fragrance speaks to oud more than it did prior. It seems to outlast the rewritten formula and much more enjoyable in my opinion. Stunning, addictive and luxurious.

Very unique and special gourmand with top notch quality!
OMG!!! The most unique gourmand fragrance I ever smelled. I just finished the whole 2ml sample within few days and I just miss it so much that I have to get a full bottle immediately, which I already did and it should be on its way. I’ve heard many good things about it for a long time and I was willing to blind buy it if I can find a discounted one from reputable sellers because I just love gourmand so much but I just never could find one. I finally got a sample to find out if I really love it and if it worth the high price tag. I ended up gave up on waiting for a discount bottle and just went to get one from their official website because I just love it this much.

At the first spray, the strong buttery opening was a little overwhelming but after couple minutes the buttery note would tone down a lot and then it starts to smell very much like French patisserie. Particularly a croissant. It’s not the cloying sugary sweet though, which I appreciate. I love gourmand sweet but I don’t like sugary cloying sweet. The sweetness is just right. Also once the buttery accord tones down, I actually love its perfect amount of the buttery and that compliment the whole vibe of it. Interestingly, I can smells the Oud in the dry down but still maintain the gourmand pastry vibe. Not animalic or barnyard at all. This is surely a masterpiece.

Performance wise, it stays closer to my skin but not an intimate skin scent at the same time. I would say it projects about 2 feet from skin until 6-7 hours mark then start fading to a skin scent. It has amazing longevity though. It lasts 12+ on my perfume eating skin. My skin tends not to grip perfumes very well (at least 2 hours less than other people). I know it’s very expensive but I do think it worth the price; the uniqueness of the fragrance, the quality ingredients you can smell from it, the performance and the bottle are all at the top notch. Especially I personally just love this scent.

The most unique gourmand ever made
Unisex

I had a difficult relationship with this special unique so called “gourmand” for long time. I loved it from the beginning, that was sure but I needed to get to know her better. I’m just not so big fan of gourmands - I like many and love some a lot but I don’t want to smell myself like many of those. There is just few gourmands which deserves the place in my collection and Sweetie Aoud is one of those.

Sweetie Aoud is not so obvious gourmand since the gourmand note is “patisserie accord” which is not any concrete food. That’s why I love it a lot. It’s rather a feeling and a smell which is a whiff of a French or Italian cafe full of delicious pastries made mainly from butter dough. The smell is a littlle bit sweet and soft buttery but the genious blend gives in the same time such a fresh vibes which I find extremely interesting.

There is a really herbal fresh opening with Bergamot and Artemisia and quickly after that follows Patisserie Accord which is the strongest during the first 30 minutes. It’s so realistic that you can just close your eyes and you can imagine yourself in front of that glass in patisserie choosing something delicious to eat with your Latte. Rose is only supportive note here and Cistus is barely detectable but those tunes down the sweetness. After this starts the main show! Next the basenotes take over your senses and the buttery note melts beautifully together with spicy, aromatic and earthy notes, Frankincense and Aoud being the prominent ones. Like all Rojas this juice is so well blended that it’s very difficult to separate notes but that makes even the gourmand perfume luxurious, polished and charming.

This is simply a stunning perfume for wintertime! Edit: actually love it a bit more in warm weather.

Oud Gourmet

Sweetie Aoud from the British House of Niche Perfumery Haute Luxury Roja London is an oriental gourmand woody floral aromatic spicy balsamic perfume. It was launched in 2015; the perfumer who signs this fragrance is Roja Dove.

At the top, Bergamot mixes with Davana to create a fresh leaf with a note of Rose, adding more freshness to the heart of this fragrance, slightly sweetened by a DELICIOUS 'Patisserie Accord'.

Which is soon warmed by an incredible accord of Noble Woods intertwined with the very present in this composition, the 'Oud safe and clean, characteristic of Roja Dove, and Incense, which bring a slightly smoky facet.

And where an exotic balsamic spicy accord of Gurjan Balsam, Cipriol and Patchouli are further tempered by Cumin and Cardamom, set on a base of Incensed Leather and Resins.
One of the best gourmand perfumes in history!

Sweetie Aoud is a beautiful and TASTY gourmand centered on 'Oud, Precious Woods, Incense and a Sweetness just right, it gives me the feeling of sweet bread or shortbread freshly taken out of the oven, a masterpiece of the master Roja Dove; and with Great Performance!
