Unknown and exclusive
This is the third fragrance in Zilli's more affordable range of four fragrances, and it seems to confirm the statement (see below) that there seem to be no bad or weak fragrances from Zilli.

Citrus-fresh is not quite correct as a description of the fragrance family for Éclat de Cuivre, especially as the grapefruit is (unfortunately) almost imperceptible.
If elemi resin is also supposed to smell fresh, lemony, woody with a green note, the incense is clearly recognizable to me at the beginning, under which it smells spicy and aromatic due to cardamom and lavender - as well as complementary probably elemi resin - in a certain way fresh.
The cedar makes the whole thing dry and woody and patchouli adds a slightly earthy spiciness.
Finally, tolu balsam, vanilla and amber add warmth and sensuality to the fragrance.

Here, too, the fragrance comes packaged in a kind of cigar box. In contrast to the heavy copper-colored cap, the elegant, angular, transparent bottle with beveled edges is only ribbed on the back and has a copper-brown-black color gradient that also matches the 'copper shard' very well.

Since I was also able to purchase this fragrance at a fifth of the list price, I once again pass on the price-performance ratio.
Beyond that, I will probably stick with Zilli fragrances.
They have very nice classic-modern, exclusive menswear.
Instead of a white T-shirt there, however, I would rather treat myself to three fragrances from the cheaper line at the list price ... because these fragrances are really good and last a long time. And since they are not very well known or widespread, they are doubly exclusive.
