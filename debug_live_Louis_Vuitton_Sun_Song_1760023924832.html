Finally Here
Took a week to arrive but was well worth the wait. Juicy, sparkling scent with great performance. Classy and easy going at the same time. I was about to pull the trigger on Neroli Portofino Forte and so glad this has popped up as one of the samples with my latest buy. Tom Ford bye bye, you are forgotten.

Sun Song has vibrant fresh slightly bitter & fizzy lemony opening that feels like hard lemon candy, right until the the point it starts to change into neroli. It is the most unisex neroli I came across. What they did with the musk balancing the orange blossom and neroli is impressive. FIY I've sampled all of the LV scents from before 2019 and I ended up loving two: L'Immensité and Sur la Route. None of these made it into my collection though.

You get solid working day wear out of it. I'd say 8-9 hours and it does seem to stick well to clothes. Can't wait for some heat to fully appreciate this one. Banger juice

Simple but Stunning
Sun Song is the absolute epitome of a simple fragrance done absolutely beautifully. Bright, tangy, musky, ever so slightly floral. (Ironically) when the sun hits your skin as you’re wearing this, it absolutely sings. Does this have a mind boggling complex scent profile? No. Does this shift dramatically on skin from opening to dry down? Not particularly. This fragrance doesn’t need to be any of that. It’s an incredible simplistic fragrance that seems timeless every time I throw it on. A true shame this one was discontinued.

Simple, yet stunning white florals with citrus fruits
Reviewing the 2025 version of Sun Song.

Simple, yet stunning, Sun Song (2025) offers few notes, but manages to masterfully showcase them all. I received an official LV sample because I was curious about this one. I do want to say that the sample glass vial is beautiful with its pink and yellow gradient (I don't know why the bottle isn't the same) like a sun setting in the horizon.

Refreshing spritz of white florals and citrus fruits, Sun Song is equal parts floral, creamy, citrusy and tangy. Neroli and orange blossom are the florals that pull the strongest on my skin. It's juicy without being too sweet, sophisticated without being uptight, it's just done well. Apart from that, I do question the enormous price tag as there are other neroli / orange blossom paired with citrus fruits fragrances on the market. Is it the prestigious aspect of LV that makes people overinflate their rating for this fragrance? Don't get me wrong, I really like the fragrance, but the enormous price tag (and that's coming from someone who has a few private lines in their collection) and the simplicity of its notes makes me wonder. It's lacking the depth and the performance I'd be expecting from a 400$+ perfume.

To be perfectly honest, I might have gone for a bottle if it wasn't for the price tag. It does balance well the sweet and tangy aspect with the sophisticated vs comforting aspect. I will acknowledge how masterfully well it's been blended but my collection is composed of citrus fragrances and white florals I could layer together for a similar (but cheaper) experience. It all comes down to whether you want to spend the money or not and where your limit is at. I might have discovered mine here.
