Warm summer nights
I agree with some other reviewers that this has some things in common with J.Lo Miami Glow, which I consider a nostalgic drugstore classic by now. But it's absolutely a huge upgrade! I always liked the poolside vibes and coconut of Miami Glow, but there is too much of the clean, musky Glow DNA in it to really love it. Oud Orange Intense doesn't have that.

It starts out with the same ultra fresh, tart, borderline ozonic fruitiness. I sense citruses (especially grapefruit) and passion fruit here. And, of course, coconut! It really makes me think of drinking a cocktail by the pool. If you don't like this fruity, fun kind of scent, this isn't for you.
But there is a warm base to it that makes it much more intense than other fragrances in this vein. I don't think that there is enough oud to warrant the name, but I get a small hint of it. Vanilla is very present, too, and some unlisted balsamic notes. Over a few hours, this turns much less fruity and more base-heavy than expected. It's an amber scent in the final drydown, the sweet, non-scratchy kind that smells like a more refined version of gummy cola bottles.

I see this as a perfume for special occasions or summer nights. Due to the ambery, woodsy warmth it can also be worn well into autumn. I would not recommend it for casual summer use, in that case, good old Miami Glow (and its lower-end dupes like Britney Spears Maui Fantasy or the lovely Jil Sander Sun Summer Edition from 2020) would honestly be the better choice.
This is quite loud and intense and perfectly unisex to me.

The only real downside is the pricing. Over 6€ per ml is just ridiculous. Oud Orange Intense smells like high quality and lasts all day, but I still wonder if one could achieve a similar effect by layering a Miami Glow adjacent freshie on a good amber fragrance.

Luxurious watercolor
When I first sniffed it, it wasn't so easy to smell something clear and specific. Definitely one of those fragrances with an "aha" effect, where you keep catching yourself saying: "Ah, now I understand, that's it...". With each new approach, you discover new facets and the fragrance becomes richer and more detailed. Rarely does a perfume challenge the imagination so actively - that's exactly what makes it so fascinating for me.

At the first spray, an opulent opening of exotic fruits, definitely with a hint of lemon and orange peel, followed by tangy coconut. After a short time, the fragrance becomes very creamy, smooth and light. The citrus fades, leaving a creamy vanilla and ambergris. The oud is only prominent in the name, it remains soft, round and harmonious, I don't smell it at all, but it is definitely there and gives the aroma structure, depth and something sensual and noble, which feels like cashmere on the skin.

The fragrance remains present for many hours, but is not at all intrusive; you feel as if you are wrapped in a small, elegant and warm cloud of fragrance. This fragrance can be compared particularly well to watercolor - fine pigments that are not so intense and loud, yet radiant and create the special magic of lightness and delicacy through multi-layeredness and complexity.

I'm particularly excited to wear it in summer - its mood and notes seem to make it perfect for warm days. But even now, in the slightly cooler temperatures, it's a real pleasure to lose yourself in this fragrance.

For lovers of subtle understatement
A 7.8 as a rating? Far too low for me. Of course, this is not a cheap fragrance, but what it gives ME personally in terms of feel-good moments makes me rave about it.
After spraying it on, the fragrance opens with a dark but elegant orange, which does not penetrate the fragrance cloud as strongly as it does on your own skin. The fragrance remains very sophisticated and high-quality, changing from a wonderful bourbon vanilla to a cozy drydown that is delicious but not too sweet. The high proportion of natural ingredients is clearly recognizable and stands out even in the niche world of entry-level brands.
The oud remains very restrained and integrated, yet promotes a certain depth without being overpowering.

For me, a fragrance for people who appreciate understatement and have outgrown the Xerjoff brutal-sillage age.

From the fruity sea to the sweet tonka base
Oud Orange Intense is a superficially sweet and fruity fragrance. Not necessarily a special DNA, but a good one.

The fragrance starts directly with these different fruity notes.
Difficult to recognize which fruity notes are active here.
In any case, there are a few pleasant citrus elements and some nice berry fruits.

As the fragrance progresses, the scent takes on a completely different kind of sweetness.
The classic tonka bean and vanilla become more and more apparent. The fruity vibes recede bit by bit.
In the transition to the tonka bean-vanilla mix, the fragrance also becomes creamier and almost "powdery".

Does much more happen?
No. Is that bad? No.
I like the scent.

The performance is solid, but not outstanding. At least that's the impression I get from this fragrance on my skin.
